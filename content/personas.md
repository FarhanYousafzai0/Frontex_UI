# PRIMARY TARGETS

These are the highest-value clients. Invest more time in personalization, prioritize proposals, and optimize win rate here.

---

## 1. The Startup Builder

**Tier:** PRIMARY | **Budget:** $50–150/hr | **Duration:** 1–6+ months

**Who they are:** Founders who have an idea — or early traction — and need a full product built fast. They want an MVP, a SaaS platform, a marketplace, or a web app that can launch, get users, and evolve. They don't just need a coder — they need someone who can make architectural decisions, plan the stack, and ship something real.

**How to spot them:** They describe a product vision, not a task list. Key signals: "MVP," "launch," "SaaS," "web app," "marketplace," "co-founder," "technical partner," "need someone to build this." They talk about users, markets, and timelines — not just features.

**Mindset:** They're moving fast and need someone who can keep up. They may have tried no-code tools or cheaper freelancers and hit a quality wall. They want someone who takes ownership of the technical side — not someone who waits for a Jira ticket. Speed and reliability matter more than perfection.

**Why they're #1:** Longest engagement windows (MVP → iteration → scaling), highest budget potential, and the most rewarding work. Once trust is built, they come back for every new feature, every pivot, every scaling challenge. One startup client can become a 6–12 month relationship.

---

## 2. The AI Automation Buyer

**Tier:** PRIMARY | **Budget:** $40–100/hr or $2,000–$15,000 fixed | **Duration:** Project-based, loyal repeat clients

**Who they are:** Business owners or operators drowning in manual, repetitive work. They're copying data between tools, manually processing leads, handling support tickets by hand, or running workflows that should be automated. They may or may not know what AI agents or n8n are — they just know their current process is eating their time.

**How to spot them:** Tool fragmentation: they mention 3+ disconnected tools (CRM, email, spreadsheets, WhatsApp, etc.). Pain language: "hours per week on," "manual," "repetitive," "we need to automate," "AI agent," "chatbot," "voice bot," "workflow." They describe pain before solution — they know it hurts but may not know the exact fix.

**Mindset:** They're operationally savvy but not necessarily technical. They want relief, not a tech lecture. They need someone who understands their business process first and then translates that into automation. ROI matters — they want to hear "this will save you 15 hours a week," not "this uses a multi-agent orchestration framework."

**Why they're top-tier:** Fastest-growing market. Clear ROI story (hours saved, costs cut). Deeply loyal once you solve their pain — they come back for more automations and refer you to their network. High volume of potential clients across every industry.

---

## 3. The Agency Overflow

**Tier:** PRIMARY | **Budget:** $40–120/hr | **Duration:** Long-term

**Who they are:** Agency owners or dev shops who sell development, design, or AI services to their own clients. They don't need something built for themselves — they need a reliable developer who can handle overflow work, white-label builds, or act as extended capacity when they land more projects than their team can deliver.

**How to spot them:** Key signals: "for our clients," "white-label," "subcontractor," "agency," "we need capacity," "overflow," "behind the scenes." They describe a role (ongoing capacity) rather than a single project. They may send you designs or specs and say "build this for our client."

**Mindset:** Reliability and consistency are everything. Their reputation is on the line — your work goes out under their brand. They need someone who delivers on time, communicates clearly, doesn't need hand-holding, and never contacts their clients directly. Trust and discretion are paramount.

**Why they're top-tier:** One relationship feeds multiple projects across their entire client base. Highest recurring revenue potential. Low sales effort once established — they bring the work to you. Compounding returns as they grow.

# SECONDARY TARGETS

Worth pursuing when primary leads are slow. Lower per-engagement value but high volume and strong growth potential.

---

## 4. The Landing Page Buyer

**Tier:** SECONDARY | **Budget:** $500–$3,000 fixed or $30–60/hr | **Duration:** 1–2 weeks

**Who they are:** Businesses, founders, or marketers who need a high-converting landing page — fast, polished, and responsive. Often tied to a time-sensitive launch: a product release, a marketing campaign, an event, or a rebrand. They may have a design ready or may need you to handle both design and development.

**How to spot them:** They mention "landing page," "one-page site," "launch page," "coming soon page," "sales page." Timeline is usually tight: "need this by next week," "launching on [date]." They care about visual quality and conversion — not backend complexity.

**Mindset:** They want it done right and done fast. They're often comparing multiple freelancers and the one who can show relevant work and promise a fast turnaround wins. They're not looking for a long-term relationship initially — but a great landing page delivered quickly builds trust that leads to bigger work.

**Growth path:** Quick wins that build your portfolio. Many landing page clients later need a full web app, e-commerce site, or automation work — making this a strong entry point into larger engagements.

---

## 5. The Product Scaler

**Tier:** SECONDARY | **Budget:** $40–100/hr | **Duration:** Ongoing

**Who they are:** Businesses or founders who have an existing app or product that works. It's live, it has users, but they've hit a wall — they need new features, performance optimization, bug fixes, or scaling improvements. They don't want to start over. They want someone who can work within what exists and make it better.

**How to spot them:** They describe what they already have before stating what they need. Language like "we have an app built in [framework]," "need to add features," "performance issues," "optimize," "scale," "take it to the next level." The emphasis is on improvement, not creation.

**Mindset:** They've invested time and money in what they have. They fear hiring someone who'll say "let's rewrite everything." They want a developer who respects the existing codebase, understands the constraints, and can improve things incrementally without breaking what works.

**Growth path:** High retention — once you prove you can work within their system, you become their go-to developer for everything. Engagements expand organically as you fix one thing and they ask you to improve the next.

# DETECTION & PRIORITY

## Priority Order (When Signals Overlap)

| Priority | Persona              | Deciding Signal                              |
| -------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | Agency Overflow      | "For our clients" or white-label language     |
| 2        | Startup Builder      | Product vision + MVP/launch signals           |
| 3        | AI Automation Buyer  | Automation pain + tool fragmentation          |
| 4        | Product Scaler       | Existing product + improvement language       |
| 5        | Landing Page Buyer   | "Landing page" + tight timeline               |

## Quick Detection Checklist

1. Read the FULL description before classifying
2. Look for the TRIGGER — what caused them to post?
3. Check if it's for THEIR CLIENTS (Agency Overflow) vs their OWN business
4. Check for product vision + startup signals (Startup Builder)
5. Check for automation pain + manual processes (AI Automation Buyer)
6. Check for existing product + improvement needs (Product Scaler)
7. Check for landing page + launch deadline (Landing Page Buyer)
8. Identify PRIMARY and SECONDARY persona if both present
9. If no persona fits, classify as "Outside Target" and skip
